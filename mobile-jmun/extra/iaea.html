INTERNATIONAL ATOMIC ENERGY AGENCY




Dear prospective delegates, faculty advisors and well-wishers


The International Atomic Energy Agency is an international organization aimed at promoting peaceful usage of nuclear energy and to inhibit its use for any military purposes.

Formed after the famous Atoms for Peace speech by President Eisenhower, the IAEA primarily focuses on the Peaceful uses, Safeguards and Safety of Nuclear energy.

Headquartered in Vienna, the Agency boasts of 162 members with ongoing operations in over 400 nuclear facilities around the world. The IAEA, along with its Director General Mohamed ElBaradei was awarded the Noble Peace Prize in 2005 for their contribution to nuclear security and peaceful usage. The current Director General of IAEA is Yukiya Amino from Japan.

At JMUN 2015, the IAEA will test the skills as well as crisis response of the delegates with a continual crisis simulation.
